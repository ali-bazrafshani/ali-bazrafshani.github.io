About me...


I am a Researcher in Robotic Mechanical Design at the Center of Advanced Systems and Technologies (CAST) at the University of Tehran, working under the supervision of Prof. Aghil Yousefi-Koma.


During the final semester of my B.Sc. at Shahid Bahonar University of Kerman, one of my mechanical design projects caught the attention of a CAST member who was part of the evaluation panel. He later invited me to join CAST ‚Äî an opportunity that marked a turning point in my academic journey.


CAST is among the leading robotics laboratories in Iran, with a strong focus on humanoid robotics. As a lab primarily oriented around dynamics and control, much of the mechanical design was previously carried out by students from other disciplines. After a few months, I found my place in the team, eventually becoming the head of mechanical design and forming my own dedicated sub-team.


At CAST, I worked on the "SURENA" humanoid robot. Driven by my personal interest, I focused heavily on improving the design of the hand. The scale and technical infrastructure of the Surena project allowed me to explore and implement significant advancements in robotic hands and grippers, such as modular mechanical design, integration of tactile sensors, and ultimately the development of a smart joint. My work on the smart joint became the foundation of my M.Sc. thesis in 2023, under the supervision of Prof. Yousefi-Koma.


My primary research interest lies in enhancing mechanical structures in robots, especially humanoids, and I am particularly focused on robotic hands and grippers, robotic manipulation where the smart joint concept was originally developed as my own idea. Additionally, I have gained valuable experience in designing other supportive mechanisms, including robotic ankles and knees.
