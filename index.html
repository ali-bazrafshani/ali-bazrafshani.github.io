About me...


I am a researcher in robotic mechanical design at the Center of Advanced Systems and Technologies (CAST) at the University of Tehran, working under the supervision of Prof. Aghil Yousefi-Koma.


During the final semester of my B.Sc. at Shahid Bahonar University of Kerman, one of my mechanical design projects caught the attention of a CAST member who was part of the evaluation panel. He later invited me to join CAST, one of the leading robotics labs in my country, with a strong focus on humanoid robotics. This opportunity marked a turning point in my academic journey.


As a lab primarily focused on dynamics and control, most of the mechanical design work at CAST had previously been done by students from other disciplines. After a few weeks, I found my place within the team, eventually becoming the head of mechanical design and forming my own dedicated sub-team, focusing primarily on the ‚ÄúSURENA‚Äù humanoid robot.


The scale and technical infrastructure of the SURENA project allowed me to explore and implement significant advancements in robotic mechanisms, especially hands and grippers, such as modular mechanical design, integration of tactile sensors, and ultimately the development of a smart joint. My work on the smart joint became the foundation of my M.Sc. thesis in 2023, under the supervision of Prof. Yousefi-Koma.


My research has focused on innovating human-like mechanisms) such as the ankle, hip, arm, and, most notably, robotic hands and grippers, where the concept of the smart joint originally emerged as my own idea.


It would be an honor if you find my work interesting and would like to get in touch.
